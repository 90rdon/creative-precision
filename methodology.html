Creative Precision / Methodology

AI governance should move as fast as AI does.

Most governance was designed for a world before AI reasoned, adapted, and acted. Precision Governance is designed for the world where it does.

The Problem

Most governance wasn't designed for AI.

AI ships in weeks. Regulations shift quarterly. Agents are entering production. But most governance still runs on annual reviews, manual checklists, and one-size-fits-all approvals. It was designed for traditional software, not for systems that reason, adapt, and act. When governance can't keep up, teams don't wait. They work around it.

The question isn't whether you need governance. It's whether your governance was designed for the AI you're actually deploying.

Precision Governance

Governance designed for AI.

Governance shouldn't slow AI down. It should be the reason AI ships with confidence. That starts with designing governance for how AI actually works, not retrofitting what was built for something else.

Four principles define the approach:

01

Govern the portfolio, not just the model.

The real governance challenge isn't any single algorithm. It's the system around it. Policy, risk, lifecycle, vendors, costs, enablement, and compliance governed as one connected system instead of scattered checklists owned by teams who never talk to each other.

02

Match the governance to the risk.

A low-risk automation and a high-stakes decision engine shouldn't go through the same process. Precision Governance scales the rigor to the risk. Lightweight where it can be. Thorough where it needs to be.

03

Make governance operational, not theoretical.

The biggest gap isn't the policy. It's the distance between the policy and Monday morning. Ready-to-deploy documentation, frameworks, and decision tools that embed governance into how work already happens.

04

Build for what's coming, not just what's here.

Models are becoming agents. Regulations are shifting quarterly. Precision Governance is built with regulatory alignment and a structure that extends into agentic AI as organizations move from tools that respond to systems that act.

Regulatory Alignment

Designed around the frameworks that matter.

Precision Governance isn't built in a vacuum. It's designed with documented alignment to the regulatory and standards frameworks that are shaping how the world governs AI.

This isn't a slide that says "we're aligned." It's documented traceability that you can put in front of an auditor, a board, or a regulator.

NIST AI RMF

The U.S. standard for identifying and managing AI risk. Precision Governance maps to its core functions: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.

ISO 42001

The international standard for AI management systems. Precision Governance aligns with its control objectives for responsible AI deployment.

EU AI Act

The most comprehensive AI regulation in the world, with enforcement deadlines already underway. Designed to support documentation, risk assessment, and transparency requirements.

Regulations are evolving alongside AI. Precision Governance is designed for both.
