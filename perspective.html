The Perspective

Three things I keep seeing
that nobody's saying.

After 14 years building AI systems inside enterprises, these are the patterns that keep showing up — in every industry, at every scale.

01

The most expensive AI projects aren't failing
because the models are wrong.

They're failing because the organization around them wasn't designed for them. The approval workflows, the risk reviews, the compliance checks — all built for a world where humans made every decision. AI doesn't need that process. It needs a different one.

The organizations pulling ahead didn't add AI to their existing operating model. They asked whether that model should still exist at all.

02

Governance is the accelerator,
not the brake.

Deloitte surveyed 3,235 AI leaders. Their finding: organizations with mature governance frameworks scale AI faster and more profitably than those without. Governance doesn't slow things down — the absence of it does. Every stalled pilot, every compliance review that takes months, every legal hold on deployment — that's what happens without a system.

The right framework turns governance from a bottleneck into the thing that gets AI into production.

03

The regulatory clock
is already running.

The EU AI Act is in force. NIST AI RMF is the U.S. standard. ISO 42001 is becoming the procurement baseline. Organizations that treat compliance as a future problem are going to discover it's a present one — with fines up to €35 million and market access at stake.

The ones moving now aren't doing it out of fear. They're doing it because early alignment is a competitive advantage, not just a legal obligation.
